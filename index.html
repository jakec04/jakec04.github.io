Hello! I’m a Chicago-based journalist and digital producer studying at DePaul University, where I’ll graduate in June 2026.

I got my start in journalism as a staff writer at The DePaulia, DePaul’s award-winning, student-run newspaper. I now serve as the paper’s Digital Managing Editor, where I combine my skills in data, web presentation, and news editing to build digital products that engage new and returning readers alike.

One of my proudest accomplishments was reporting on DePaul’s 17-day protest encampment, during which I pioneered the live blog format the paper now uses regularly for breaking news and sports. That coverage earned our team a Chicago Headline Club Peter Lisagor Award. I’ve also led a full website redesign and grew our newsletter’s audience sixfold.

Currently, I’m a newsletter intern at Block Club Chicago, where I help shape email products that serve the city’s diverse neighborhoods. I write and build daily newsletters and contribute to long-term projects to improve verticals like 'Proud City' and 'Block Club Eats'.

Outside of the newsroom, I enjoy exploring how data can drive both news and features. I’m earning a certificate in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) at DePaul to deepen my data reporting skills, and I’m an avid reader of archival newspapers. I’m excited to bring my passion for local journalism, data, and audience engagement to any newsroom I join.
